## Christian Spiritual Formation Complementary Major (28-30 credit hours)

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

This major is designed to complement any major offered by the University.  Its aim is to aid students in becoming complete and mature disciples of Jesus Christ by integrating Christian faith with academic knowledge in order to produce Christian professionals who are equipped to serve the Church and the world in whatever their chosen profession or vocation may be.

## Major Requirements

## Spiritual Formation Core (9 credit hours):

- RLGN 1100, Introduction to Spiritual Formation, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 4960, Capstone Course:  Spiritual Formation Through Work, Calling, and Holiness, 3 credit hours

## And either

- RLGN 2410, Where am I? Faith and Cosmology, 3 credit hours

## OR

- RLGN 2430, Who am I? Theological Anthropology, 3 credit hours

## Any 3 Interdisciplinary Courses (7-9 credit hours):

- PSYC/SOCI, 3500, Thought Life and Spiritual Growth, 2 credit hours
- ENGL 2580, The Spiritual Practice of Writing, 3 credit hours
- MUSC 3150, Art, Faith, and Culture, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 3200, Christian Living in a Postmodern World, 3 credit hours
- PACT 2400, Principles and Practices of Conflict Transformation, 2 credit hours

## One course from EACH of the four categories below (12 credit hours):

## Scripture:

- BIBL 2150, Theological Interpretation of the Bible, 3 credit hours or
- BIBL/RLGN 3000, The Bible, Christians, and Biblical Interpretation (WI ), 3 credit hours

## Tradition:

- RLGN /HIST 3060, History of Christianity, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 2270, Church of God Heritage, 4 credit hours
- HIST 3540, History of the Civil Rights Movement, 3 credit hours

## Reason:

- RLGN 3120, Current Issues in Christian Ethics, 3 credit hours
- RLGN/PHIL 3250, Ethics and Morality for Professionals, 3 credit hours
- PHIL 2000, Introduction to Philosophy, 3 credit hours

## Experience:

- RLGN 2310, Faith , Fantasy, and Christian Formation, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 3100, Christian Understandings of Human Experience, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 3530, Celtic Spirituality, 3 credit hours. (will be taught every other May beginning in 2019.)
