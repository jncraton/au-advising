## Bible &amp; Religion Major (36 credit hours)                                                                                        \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

Courses in this major are drawn from the related areas of theology, Bible, and religion as students explore the biblical sources of Christian religion and its theology, practice, and relation to life and culture.  This major is especially valuable for pre-seminary students.

## Major Requirements:

- BIBL 2 000, Introduction to the Bible, 3 credit hours
- BIBL 2050, Methods in Biblical Exegesis, 3 credit hours **
- RLGN 2000, Introduction to Religion, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 2060, History of Christianity, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 2130, Introduction to Christian Ethics, 3 credit hours**
- RLGN 2150, Hermeneutics: The Practice of Interpreting, 3 credit hours **
- RLGN 3040, Introduction to Christian Theology, 3 credit hours**
- RLGN 3320, A World of Religions, 3 credit hours***
- RLGN 3300, Beginnings of Christianity, 3 credit hours**
- Upper Division Bible or Religion Course, 3 credit hours
- Hebrew Bible Exegetical Course, 3 credit hours
- New Testament Exegetical Course, 3 credit hours
- **BIBL 2000 is a prerequisite for this course
- ***Upper division standing required
- BIBL 2000, Introduction to the Bible, is a Biblical Literacy course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- RLGN 3320, A World of Religions, is a Global/Intercultural course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- Biblical Greek (BIBL 2210 and BIBL 2220) and Biblical Hebrew (BIBL 2110, 2120) may NOT be applied toward the major, but they do meet the Liberal Arts Program's Global/Intercultural Ways of Knowing: Ancient Foreign Languages.

## Religion Minor (15 credit hours)

- RLGN 2000, Introduction to Religion, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 2060, History of Christianity, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 3320, A World of Religions, 3 credit hours

Remaining hours from RLGN-captioned courses not applied to liberal arts requirements.

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.
