## Social Work Major (64 credit hours)

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

The social work major is accredited by the Council on Social Work Education (CSWE) and prepares students to become generalist social work practitioners in entry-level human service positions or for graduate study in social work. Graduates are eligible to apply for credentialing as beginning-level social work practitioners in most states. Students must apply to this program after completion of the 8 credit hours of prerequisite coursework.

## Major Requirements:

## 8 credit hours of pre-requisites from:

- SOCI 2010, Introduction to Sociology, 3 credit hours or SOCI 2020, Social Problems, 3 credit hours
- SOWK 2000, Introduction to Social Welfare and Social Work, 3 credit hours
- SOWK 2100, Introduction to Field Experience, 2 credit hours

## 19 credit hours of Social Work foundation courses from:

- BIOL 2040, Personal and Community Health, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2100, American National Government, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 2000, General Psychology, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 2440, Applied Statistics/Intro Research, 4 credit hours
- SOCI 3100, Human Sexuality, 3 credit hours
- SOCI 2450, Introduction to Issues in Race and Ethnicity, 3 credit hours

## 37 credit hours of Social Work core courses from:

- SOCI 3700, Introduction to Social Research, 4 credit hours
- SOWK 2200, Human Behavior and Social Environment, 4 credit hours
- SOWK 3100, Social Welfare Policy, 4 credit hours
- SOWK 4350, Program Planning &amp; Grant Writing, 3 credit hours
- SOWK 4710, Social Work Practice, 4 credit hours
- SOWK 4720, Social Work Practice with Families and Groups, 3 credit hours
- SOWK 4730, Social Work Practice with Communities and Organizations, 3 credit hours
- SOWK 4850, Social Work Practicum, 12 credit hours

Internship experiences in social agencies are built into SOWK 2100 (36 hours), SOWK 2200 (20 hours), and SOWK 4710 (20 hours). The practicum (SOWK 4850) consists of a full-time block field placement over the entire semester (442 hours).

- PSYC 2000, General Psychology, is a Social &amp; Behavioral Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program
- PSYC 2440, Applied Statistics/Intro Research, is a Quantitative Reasoning course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- SOCI 2010, Introduction to Sociology, is a Social &amp; Behavioral Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program
- SOCI 2020, Social Problems, is a Social &amp; Behavioral Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- SOCI 2450, Introduction to Issues in Race and Ethnicity is a Civil Discourse and Critical Reasoning course in the Liberal Arts Program. It is also a Global/Intercultural Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program
- SOCI 3700, Introduction to Social Research, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- SOWK 4850, Social Work Practicum, is a Speaking Intensive course and fulfills the Experiential Ways of Knowing requirement in the Liberal Arts Program.

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University. Proposed course sequence:
