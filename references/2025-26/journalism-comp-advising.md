## Journalism Complementary Major (30 credit hours)

## Major Requirements:

- COMM 2000, Media and Society, 3 credit hours
- COMM 2010, The Communication Professional, 1 credit hour
- COMM 2130, Writing for the Media, 3 credit hours
- COMM 2200, Visual Communication, 3 credit hours
- COMM 3130, Advanced Newswriting and Reporting, 3 credit hours
- COMM 3200, Communication Ethics, 3 credit hours
- COMM 3230, Advanced Feature and Magazine Writing, 3 credit hours
- COMM 4000, Communication Law, 3 credit hours
- COMM 4800, Internship, 2 credit hours

## 3 credit hours from:

- COMM 2850, Andersonian Staff , 1 credit hour

## Remaining hours from these electives:

- COMM 2140, Audio/Video/Cinema Production for Journalists and PR Practitioners, 3 credit hours
- COMM 3330, Communication Design, 3 credit hours
- COMM 3370, Communicating through Social Media, 3 credit hours

## Proposed course sequence:

Freshman:

COMM 2000, 2010; COMM 2130, 2200

Sophomore:

COMM 2850; COMM 2850

Junior:

COMM 3200, 3230; COMM 2850, 4000

Senior:

COMM 2140/3330/3370; COMM 3130, 4800

- COMM 2130, Writing for the Media, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- COMM 3230, Advanced Feature and Magazine Writing, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.

Through the mentoring of experienced and caring faculty, journalism students gain the understanding that communication is a sacred trust, privilege, and obligation. Students develop the capacity to make sound and discerning judgments about communication content, technique, and effectiveness. In the end, students gain not only professional competence but also personal integrity and maturity, qualities that are strongly valued and encouraged at Anderson University.
