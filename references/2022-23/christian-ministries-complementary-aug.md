## Christian Ministries Complementary Major (28 credit hours)

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

The Christian Ministries major is designed to prepare students to serve as Christian leaders and servants in the world.  It offers undergraduate study of ministry which may lead to:

1. Lay ministry while pursuing another profession.
2. A position of leadership in ministry in a local congregation, a church-related organization, or a related field.
3. Graduate study in ministry.  This major is intended to be a first step in a plan of lifelong learning for ministry.

## Departmental Core Courses:

- BIBL 20 00, Introduction to the Bible, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 2130, Introduction to Christian Ethics, 3 credit hours **
- RLGN 2150, Hermeneutics: The Practice of Interpreting, 3 credit hours **
- RLGN 3040, Introduction to Christian Theology, 3 credit hours **
- RLGN 3060, History of Christianity, 3 credit hours

## Ministry Core Courses:

- CMIN 2000, Introduction to Church Ministry, 3 credit hours

## 3 hours from one of the following:

- CMIN 3050, Homiletics, 3 credit hours **
- CMIN 3080, Pastoral Care and Counseling, 3 credit hours ***
- CMIN 3910, Ministry Seminar, 3 credit hours***

## At least 1 hour from:

- CMIN 2810, Ministry Exploration, 1 hour
- CMIN 3340, The Community of Faith in an Urban Culture, 3 credit hours**** (instructor's consent required)
- CMIN 4810, Internship in Ministry, 1-4 hours (arranged)***
- 3 additional h ours from CMIN-captioned courses
- 3 additional hours from any upper-division courses offered by the department

## These courses from the list above also meet the following Liberal Arts Requirements:

- BIBL 2000, Introduction to the Bible, is a Biblical Literacy course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- CMIN 3910, Ministry Seminar, meets the Speaking Intensive requirement for the Liberal Arts.
- Either CMIN 4810 ( Internship in Ministry) or CMIN 4850 (Ministry Practicum) meets the Experiential Requirement for the Liberal Arts.
- CMIN 4250, Theology in a Ministry Context, applies to the Liberal Arts upper-division writing-intensive requirement.  You could also use this course, as an upper-division departmental elective.

**BIBL 2000 is a prerequisite for this course

***BIBL 2000 and/or CMIN 2000 are the prerequisite(s) for this course.

****CMIN 3340 is offered every May term.
