## Dance Performance Major (54 credit hours)

Prepares students for performance, theatre, and choreography careers, graduate degrees in; dance, performance &amp; choreography, history, global dance/global studies, dance ministry, dance anthropology, cultural studies, and/or performance studies.

## Major Requirements:

## Required Courses 22 credit hours:

- DANC 1580, Dance Improvisation I, 1 credit hour
- DANC 1590, Dance Composition I, 2 credit hours
- DANC 3000, Global Dance Forms, 3 credit hours
- DANC 3020, Dance History II, 3 credit hours
- DANC 4500, Dance Anatomy and Kinesiology, 3 credit hours
- NURS 1210 Nutrition OR PEHS 1450 First Aid, 2 credit hours
- 3 hours from DANC 1220-4220 (modern technique)
- 3 hours from DANC 1420-4420 (ballet technique)
- 2 hours from DANC 1320-4320 (jazz technique)

## Dance Performance Track 32 credit hours:

- DANC 2110, Musical Theatre Dance Forms, 1 credit hour
- DANC 2580 Dance Improvisation II, 1 credit hour
- DANC 2590, Dance Composition II, 2 credit hours
- DANC 3010, Dance History I, 3 credit hours
- DANC 3050, Dance Pedagogy OR DANC 3060, Movement Analysis, 3 credit hours
- DANC 3530, Partnering for Ballet and Modern Dance, 1 credit hour
- DANC 3580, Dance Improvisation III, 1 credit hour
- DANC 3590, Dance Composition III, 2 credit hours
- DANC 4590, Dance Composition IV, 2 credit hours
- DANC 4910, Seminar in Professional Praxis for Dancers, 2 credit hours
- 3 hours from DANC 2220-4220 (modern technique)
- 3 hours from DANC 2420-4420 (ballet technique)
- 6 hours of DANC 1150
- 2 hours of DANC 1160

Participation in off-campus professional workshops, seminars, conferences related to field. All Dance majors and minors require an audition for admittance to the Dance Program.

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.

## Dance Pedagogy Major (54 credit hours)

Prepares students to teach in public schools, private studios, institutions of higher education, or pursue graduate study in dance education. This path of study helps students prepare for (or seek concurrent) CIDE [Certification in Dance Education], which is necessary for work in K-12 classrooms.

## Major Requirements:

## Required Courses 22 credit hours:

- DANC 1580, Dance Improvisation I, 1 credit hour
- DANC 1590, Dance Composition I, 2 credit hours
- DANC 3000, Global Dance Forms, 3 credit hours
- DANC 3020, Dance History II, 3 credit hours
- DANC 4500, Dance Anatomy and Kinesiology, 3 credit hours
- NURS 1210 Nutrition OR PEHS 1450 First Aid, 2 credit hours
- 3 hours from DANC 1220-4220 (modern technique)
- 3 hours from DANC 1420-4420 (ballet technique)
- 2 hours from DANC 1320-4320 (jazz technique)

## Dance Pedagogy Track 32 credit hours:

- 1 additional hour from DANC 2220-4420 (modern technique)
- 1 additional hour from DANC 2420-4420 (ballet technique)
- 1 additional hour from DANC 2330, 3320 (jazz technique)
- 4 hours of DANC 1150, Dance Repertory, 1 credit hour
- 2 hours of DANC 1160, Dance Production, 1 credit hour
- 2 hours of DANC 2850 Practice in Teaching
- DANC 2580, Dance Improvisation II, 1 credit hour
- DANC 2590, Dance Composition II, 2 credit hours
- DANC 3010, Dance History I, 3 credit hours
- DANC 3050, Dance Pedagogy I, 3 credit hours
- DANC 3055, Dance Pedagogy II, 3 credit hours
- DANC 3590, Dance Composition III, 2 credit hours
- DANC 4590, Dance Composition IV, 2 credit hours
- EDUC 2110, Educational Psychology: Learning in the Schools, 3 credit hours
- 2 hours of additional related courses

Participation in off-campus professional workshops, seminars, conferences related to field. Example NDEO, NASD, ADTA.  All Dance majors and minors require an audition for admittance to the Dance Program

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.

## Dance Science Major (54 credit hours)

Prepares students for pursuing careers in the dance, therapy, and health industries. It serves well in preparation for EMT or Paramedic certification, massage therapy, physical/personal trainer, or graduate study in physical therapy, biomechanics, dance kinesiology, or somatic/expressive/dance therapy.

## Major Requirements:

## Required Courses 22 credit hours:

- DANC 1580, Dance Improvisation I, 1 credit hour
- DANC 1590, Dance Composition I, 2 credit hours
- DANC 3000, Global Dance Forms, 3 credit hours
- DANC 3020, Dance History II, 3 credit hours
- DANC 4500, Anatomy and Kinesiology, 3 credit hours
- NURS 1210 Nutrition OR PEHS 1450 First Aid, 2 credit hours
- 3 hours from DANC 1220-4220 (modern technique)
- 3 hours from DANC 1420-4420 (ballet technique)
- 2 hours from DANC 1320-4320 (jazz technique)

## Dance Science Track 32 credit hours:

- 2 hours from DANC 1150, Dance Repertory, 1 credit hour
- 1 hours from DANC 1160, Dance Production, 1 credit hour
- CHEM 1000, Introduction to Chemistry, 4 credit hours
- PYSC 2000, General Psychology, 3 credit hours
- SOCI 2010, Introduction to Sociology, 3 credit hours
- MATH 2120, Introductory Statistics with Applications, 4 credit hours
- PHYS 2140, General Physics I (Algebra based), 4 credit hours
- DANC 3060, Movement Analysis, 3 credit hours
- DANC 4060, Motor Control, 3 credit hours
- 5 hours of approved related courses

Participation in off-campus professional workshops, seminars, conferences related to field. Example IADMS, PAMA, ADTA, or taking professional development courses (Trinity Laban).  All Dance majors and minors require an audition for admittance to the Dance Program

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.
