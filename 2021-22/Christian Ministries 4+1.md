## Christian Ministries Major to Masters of Divinity (46 credit hours)

The Christian Ministries major is designed to prepare students to serve as Christian leaders and servants in the world.  It offers undergraduate study of ministry which may lead to:

1. Lay ministry while pursuing another profession.
2. A position of leadership in ministry in a local congregation, a church-related organization, or a related field.
3. Further study in ministry.  This combined major is intended to be a step in a plan of lifelong learning for ministry.

## Major Requirements:

## Departmental Core (24 hours):

- BIBL 20 00, Introduction to the Bible, 3 credit hours
- BIBL 2050, Methods in Biblical Exegesis, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 2000, Introduction to Religion, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 206 0, History of Christianity, 3 credit hours (CHIS 5100)
- RLGN 2130, Introduction to Christian Ethics, 3 credit hours (THST 5500)
- RLGN 2150, Hermeneutics: The Practice of Interpreting, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 3040, Introduction to Christian Theology, 3 credit hours
- RLGN 3 300, Beginnings of Christianity, 3 credit hours

## Ministry Core (19 hours):

- CMIN 2000, Introduction to Christian Ministry, 3 credit hours
- CMIN 3050, Homiletics, 3 credit hours (PAST 5500)
- CMIN 3080, Pastoral Care and Counseling, 3 credit hours (PAST 6370)
- CMIN 3910 , Ministry Seminar, 3 credit hours (PAST 7470)
- CMIN 4250, Theology in a Ministry Context, 3 credit hours
- CMIN 48 10, Internship in Ministry, 1-4 credit hours

Majors must take 3 credit hours additional from courses offered by the department.

- BIBL 2000, Introduction to the Bible, is a Biblical Literacy course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- CMIN 4250, Theology in a Ministry Context, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
