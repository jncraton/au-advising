## Nursing Major (83 credit hours)

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

The BSN focuses on developing the student for beginning practice as a member of the profession who is a provider, educator, scholar, designer, manager, and coordinator of care. Students are taught to design innovative nursing care for individuals, families, communities, and populations with complex health states in a variety of health-care settings across the lifespan.

## Major Requirements:

The School of Nursing strongly recommends NURS 2130 be taken before entering the major.

Skills &amp; Procedures Boot Camp (NURS 2170) is required the fall of the sophomore year with the exception of successfully completing the check off.

## 60 hours in Nursing:

- NURS 2231, Fundamentals I, 5 credit hours
- NURS 2241, Fundamentals II, 5 credit hours
- NURS 2250, Foundational Strategies for NCLEX Success, 1 credit hour
- NURS 2270, Pathophysiological Basis for Pharmacology in Nursing, 4 credit hours
- NURS 2340, Community Health Nursing, 2 credit hours
- NURS 3351, Adult Health I, 6 credit hours
- NURS 3361, Adult Health II, 6 credit hours
- NURS 3391, Evidence-Based Practice, 3 credit hours
- NURS 4451, Adult Health III, 7 credit hours
- NURS 4470, Seminar in Nursing, 4 credit hours
- NURS 4510, Professional Nursing Practice
- NURS 4521, Child Health, 4 credit hours
- NURS 4950, Intercultural Capstone, 4 credit hours
- NURS 4960, Leadership Capstone, 4 credit hours
- NURS 4970, Clinical Capstone, 2 credit hours

## 23 credit hours in supportive sciences:

- BIOL 2230, Microbes and Disease, 4 credit hours
- BIOL 2410, Human Anatomy and Physiology, 4 credit hours
- BIOL 2420, Human Anatomy and Physiology, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 2000, General Psychology, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 2510, Developmental Psychology, 4 credit hours
- CHEM 1000, Introduction to Chemistry, 4 credit hours
- CHEM 1000, Introduction to Chemistry, is a Scientific Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- NURS 3390, Nursing Research, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- NURS 4450, Partial Self Care Agency I, is a Speaking Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- NURS 4470, Seminar in Nursing, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- PSYC 2000, General Psychology, is a Social &amp; Behavioral Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
