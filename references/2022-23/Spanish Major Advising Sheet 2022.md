## Spanish Major (43-45 credit hours)

\_\_\_

A major in Spanish offers students a triple focus: language, culture, and literature. Upon completion, majors will be equipped to comprehend and interpret written and spoken language, express themselves orally and in writing, have a fundamental understanding of the literature and cultures of the Hispanic world, and value other cultures as responsible global citizens. Students are prepared linguistically for graduate study as well as careers in areas such as -but not limited to -business, education, law, health care, social services, missionary service, journalism, law enforcement, or public service.

Students must complete 33 SPAN hours including the classes listed below. Note: students must also complete either: 6 SPAN hours through an approved study abroad experience; OR 5 SPAN hours through an approved intensive experience and take SPAN 3240 for at least 1 hour.

- SPAN 2020, Intermediate Spanish II, 4 credit hours
- SPAN 3010, Spanish Composition, 4 credit hours
- SPAN 3020, Spanish Conversation, 4 credit hours
- SPAN 3140, Spanish Phonetics, 3 credit hours
- One civilization course, typically:
- -- SPAN 3400, Spanish Civilization and Culture, 3 credit hours OR
- -- SPAN 3440, Latin American Civilization, 3 credit hours
- One 4-hour literature course at the 4xxx level, typically:
- -- SPAN 4350 or 4360, Survey of Latin American Literature I or II, 4 credit hours OR
- -- SPAN 4370 or 4380, Survey of Peninsular Literature I or II, 4 credit hours
- Other SPAN-captioned coursework beyond 2010 until at least 33 SPAN hours are attained, for example:
- -- SPAN 31XX, Spanish for the Professions, credit hours vary
- -- SPAN 3200, Advanced Spanish Grammar and Translation, 3 credit hours
- -- SPAN 3240, Strategies for Advanced Spanish Proficiency, 1-2 credit hours

## Students must complete 10-12 additional hours from the following areas:

- MLAN 3500, The Immersive Experience, 1 credit hour (C/NC)
- MLAN 4900, Senior Seminar in Cultural Studies, 3 credit hours
- A two-semester sequence (6-8 hours) in another modern language, typically:
- -- FREN 1010 and 1020, Elementary French I and II, 6-8 credit hours OR
- -- GERM 1010 and 1020, Elementary German I and II, 6-8 credit hours

The proposed course sequence will vary based upon a student's initial language placement. The following pages include sample course sequences for students placing into these levels as incoming first year students:

- SPAN 2010 (w/ semester study abroad …. summer and full year options also available )
- SPAN 2020 (w/ semester study abroad…. summer and full year options also available)
- SPAN 3010 (w/ semester study abroad…. summer and full year options also availabl e)

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.
