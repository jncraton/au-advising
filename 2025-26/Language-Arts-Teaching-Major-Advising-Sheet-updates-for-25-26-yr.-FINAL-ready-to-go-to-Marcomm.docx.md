English Language Arts Teaching Major (45 credit hours in English and 43 credit hours of Secondary Education Requirements through the Education Department)

The English language arts teaching major is designed to prepare the student for teaching in grades 5-12. It includes a 39-hour core of courses with English, MLAN, and speech captions, thus qualifying the student to teach English and speech courses in grades 5-12, and 6 additional hours of advanced writing.

## Required courses (39 credit hours):

- ENGL 2220, Global Literature, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 2400, Introduction to Literature, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3000, English Grammar and Style, 3 credit hours
- MLAN 2000, Language and Society, 4 credit hours
- ENGL 3140, Writing and Digital Media OR ENGL 3160, Professional Writing and Editing, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3320, Shakespeare, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3540, Studies in Early British Literature, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3560, Studies in Later British Literature, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3570, Studies in American Literature, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3580, American Ethnic Literature, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3590, Teaching Young Adult Literature, 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3880, Tutor Practicum, 1 credit hour
- ENGL 4700, Methods of Teaching English* (co-requisite), 3 credit hours
- COMM 4750, Strategies for Teaching Speech* (co-requisite), 1 credit hour

Note: ENGL 4700 and COMM 4750 should be taken prior to your professional semester. Note also that ENGL 4700 is offered in the fall only.

## One course from each of the following sets (6 credit hours):

- ENGL 3110 (Creative Writing Fiction) OR ENGL 3120 (Creative Writing Poetry) OR ENGL 2580 (Spiritual Practice of Writing), 3 credit hours
- ENGL 3180 (Composing Non-fiction) OR ENGL 3190 (Composing  Arguments), 3 credit hours

In addition to these English courses, students pursuing a teaching major also must meet the Secondary Education requirements listed below.

- EDUC 2110, Educational Psychology: Learning in the Schools, 3 credit hours
- SPED 2400, Introduction to Mild Disabilities, 3 credit hours
- EDUC 2460, Teaching in a Diverse Society, 3 credit hours
- EDUC 2520, Technology in the Classroom, 2 credit hours
- EDUC 2860, Practicum in Secondary Education, 2 credit hour
- EDUC 3000, Teaching Literacy Skills in Middle and High School Content Areas, 3 credit hours
- EDUC 3100, The Middle School in the United States, 3 credit hours
- EDUC 3120, Educational Assessment for the Classroom Teacher, 3 credit hours
- EDUC 4710, Management, Organization, and Methods in the Secondary Classroom, 3 credit hours
- EDUC 4715, Professional Semester Preparation Seminar - Secondary &amp; P-12, 1 credit hour
- EDUC 4930, Leadership Seminar in Character Education, 2 credit hours Student Teaching:
- EDUC 4010, Student Teaching, 10-22 credit hours

## Liberal Arts Requirements:

- Majors are required to take HIST 2110 or 2120 for the Civic Ways of Knowing liberal arts requirement.
- Majors are required to take ENGL 3590 for the Aesthetic Ways of Knowing liberal arts requirements.
- Writing Intensive courses: ENGL 3110/3120, ENGL 3180/3190, ENGL 3580, ENGL 4700
- ENGL 3580 and SPED 2400 satisfy the Civil Discourse and Critical Reasoning requirement
- Speaking Intensive courses: ENGL 2220
- EDUC 4710 is both a writing intensive and speaking intensive course.
