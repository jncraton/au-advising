## Cybersecurity Major (64 credit hours)

The Cybersecurity program is designed for students who seek to defend against cyberthreats in the realms of industry and public service. Cybersecurity majors seek to be the "good guys", similar to firefighters or policemen, defending individuals, customers, and the public against today's ever present cyberattacks. Courses acquaint students with information assurance, server and network environments, cryptography, cyber-related public policy, and cyberwarfare. Majors in Cybersecurity typically pursue careers in industry for a wide variety of companies, in a security operations center, or in local, state, or federal government service.

## Major Requirements:

## Foundation Courses (31 credit hours):

- CPSC 1080, Introduction to Computing, 3 credit hours
- CPSC 2020, Fundamentals of Computational Thinking and Programming, 4 credit hours
- CPSC 2080, Introduction to Cybersecurity, 3 credit hours
- CPSC 2300, System Administration, 3 credit hours
- MATH 2120, Introductory Statistics with Applications, 3 credit hours
- MATH 2200/CPSC 2250, Discrete Mathematical Structures, 4 credit hours
- POSC 2020, Introduction to World Politics, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2200, Public Policy, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2400, Social Science Research Methods, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2420, Applied Statistics Lab, 2 credit hours

## National Security Policy Courses (6 credit hours):

- POSC/CRIM 3350, Homeland Security, 3 credit hours
- POSC 3370, Intelligence and Security Studies, 3 credit hours

## Ethics Course (3 credit hours):

- PHIL/RLGN 3250, Ethics and Morality for Professionals, 3 credit hours

## Professional Core (18 credit hours):

- CPSC 2150, Technology and Society, 2 credit hours
- CPSC 3410, Computer Networks, 4 credit hours
- CPSC 3480, Network Security, 3 credit hours
- CPSC 4060, Penetration Testing, 3 credit hours
- CPSC 4070, Cybersecurity Analysis and Digital Forensics, 3 credit hours
- Any 3 credit hours from the following:
- CPSC 4480, Technical Certification, 1 credit hours
- CPSC 4820, Cybersecurity Internship, 2-4 credit hours

## Electives (6 credit hours) chosen from:

- CPSC 2000 and above
- CRIM 2520, Introduction to Criminal Justice, 3 credit hours
- POSC 3250, Constitutional Law, 3 credit hours
- POSC 3310, Political Violence &amp; Terrorism, 3 credit hours
- PSYC/CRIM 3080, Forensic Psychology 2 credit hours
- PSYC 3330, Cognitive Psychology, 3 credit hours

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.
