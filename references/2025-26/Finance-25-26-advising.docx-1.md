## Finance Major (66 credit hours)

Courses in Finance are designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of financial management, investment  strategies,  and  the  operation  of  financial  markets.  This  program  prepares  students  for careers  in  corporate  finance,  investment  banking,  financial  planning,  and  other  financial  services. Additionally, it lays the groundwork for graduate study in finance and related fields.

## Business Core Requirements (48-hours):

- ACCT 2010 Principles of Accounting I, 3 credit hours
- ACCT 2020 Principles of Accounting II, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 1050 Business as a Profession, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 2550 Business Communications, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 2310 Spreadsheet Analytics, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 2450 Principles of Business Analytics, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 2510 Principles of Finance, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 2710 Principles of Management, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 2810 Principles of Marketing, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 3120 Global Business, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 3420 Business Law, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 4500 Strategic Management, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 4800, Business Internship, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 4910 Senior Seminar in Business, 3 credit hours
- ECON 2010 Principles of Macroeconomics, 3 credit hours
- ECON 2020 Principles of Microeconomics, 3 credit hours

## M ajor Requirements (18 hours):

- BSNS 3150, Financial Planning, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 3350, Corporate Finance, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 4150, Investments, 3 credit hours
- BSNS 4160, Portfolio Management, 3 credit hours
- ECON 3410, Money &amp; Banking, 3 credit hours

## Plus 3 elective credit hours from:

- BSNS 3850, Practicum in Business, 1-3 credit hours
- BSNS 4240, Topics in Business and Economics, 1-3 credit hours
- BSNS 4320, Funding and Pricing New Ventures, 1-3 credit hours
- BSNS 4800, Business Internship, 1-4 credit hours
- ACCT 4020, Federal Tax, 3 credit hours

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.
