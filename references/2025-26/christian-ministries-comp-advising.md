## Christian Ministries Complementary Major (28 credit hours) *

## Departmental Core Courses:

- BIBL 2000, Introduction to the Bible, 3 credit hours (offered each semester)
- RLGN 2130, Introduction to Christian Ethics, 3 credit hours (offered semester one)**
- RLGN 2150, Hermeneutics: The Practice of Interpreting, 3 credit hours (offered semester one)**
- RLGN 3040, Introduction to Christian Theology, 3 credit hours (offered semester two)**
- RLGN 3420, American Religious History, 3 credit hours (offered semester two)

## Ministry Core Courses:

- CMIN 2000, Introduction to Church Ministry, 3 credit hours (offered both semesters) and,

## 3 hours from one of these courses:

- •CMIN 3050, Homiletics, 3 credit hours (offered semester one)**
- •CMIN 3080, Pastoral Care and Counseling, 3 credit hours (offered semester two)***
- CMIN 3910, Ministry Seminar, 3 credit hours (offered both semesters)***

## At least 1 hour from:

- •CMIN 3340, The Community of Faith in an Urban Culture, 3 credit hours (instructor's consent required) or,
- •CMIN 4810, Internship in Ministry, 1-4 hours (arranged)***

- 3 additional hours from CMIN-captioned courses
- 3 additional hours from any upper-division courses offered by the department

- ***BIBL 2000 and/or CMIN 2000 is/are the prerequisite(s) for this course

The Christian Ministries major is designed to prepare students to serve as Christian leaders and servants in the world.  It offers undergraduate study of ministry which may lead to:

<!-- image -->

1. Lay ministry while pursuing another profession. 2. A position of leadership in ministry in a local congregation, a church-related organization, or a related field. 3. Graduate study in ministry.  This major is intended to be a first step in a plan of lifelong learning for ministry.
