## Elementary Education Major (83 credit hours)

The Elementary Education Teaching major is designed to prepare students for teaching in an elementary classroom and meets the licensure requirements for the state of Indiana.

## Liberal Arts Requirements:

- BIOL 1000, Principle s of Modern Biology, is a Scientific Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- EDUC 2110, Educational Psychology, is a Social and Behavioral Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- EDUC 3120, Educational Assessment for the Classroom Teacher, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- EDUC 4120, Organizational and Management for the Elementary Classroom, is a Writing and Speaking Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- ENGL 3590, Teaching Diversity through Young Adult Literature, is an Aesthetic Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- HIST 2000, History of World Civilization, is a Civic Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- HIST 2110, American Civilization I, is a Civic Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- MATH 1100, Elementary MathematicsNumeration Systems, is a Quantitative Reasoning course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- MUED 2110, Music for the Elementary Classroom, is an Aesthetic Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts.

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.

## Special Education Minor -Mild Intervention:

- SPED 2400, Introduction to Mild Disabilities, 3 credit hours (required course for all elementary education candidates)
- SPED 2500, Technology for the Diverse Learner, 3 credit hours
- SPED 2550, Characteristics of Emotional and Behavioral Disorders of Children &amp; Youth, 3 credit hours
- SPED 3000, Classroom Instruction for Mild Disabilities, 3 credit hours (required course for all elementary education candidates)
- SPED 3200, Special Education Assessment for the Classroom Teacher, 3 credit hours (required course for all elementary education candidates)

Embedded in the elementary education major is a 12-credit reading concentration that can serve as the required concentration for state licensing. Elementary education majors are also strongly encouraged to complete another minor or concentration such as special education which leads to an additional license area.
