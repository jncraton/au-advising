## International Relations Major (42-43 credit hours)

The International Relations major is designed to transform students with an interest in or concern for international affairs into graduates who can understand, evaluate, manage, and shape political events and governmental actions in a manner consistent with a Christian faith perspective. The major involves the study of the international political system and processes to explain outcomes and policies that promote peace and justice. Graduates will be prepared for successful careers in government, politics, law, non-profits, teaching, journalism, business, and more.

## Major Requirements:

## Foundational Courses (15 credit hours):

- POSC 2020, Introduction to World Politics, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2200, Public Policy, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2400, Social Science Research Methods, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2580, Principles of Geography, 3 credit hours
- MATH 2120, Introductory Statistics with Applications, 3 credit hours

## Advanced Courses (12 credit hours):

- POSC 3300, International Security, 3 credit hours
- POSC 3400, Comparative Politics, 3 credit hours
- POSC 3510, International Political Economy, 3 credit hours
- One from: POSC 3310, 3320, 3330, 3360, 3450

## Experiential Courses (4-5 credit hours) from:

- POSC 2840, Model Diplomatic Simulation Conferences, 1 credit hour (may be repeated)
- POSC 4820, International Internship, 1-4 credit hours

## Electives (9-10 credit hours) from:

- ECON 3210
- HIST 3190, 3220, 3240, 3250, 3260, 3300, 3360, 3370, 3520
- Study abroad or intensive experience approved by the department
- SPAN 2010 or above
- Language courses taken at institutions or programs approved by the department

## Senior Capstone (1 hour):

- POSC 4930, Senior Seminar, 1 credit hour

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.
