## Visual Communication Design Major (54 credit hours)

## Major Requirements:

- ARTH 2100, History of Art, Design, and Visual Culture in the Modern World, 3 credit hours
- ARTH 3010, Ancient to Medieval Art, 3 credit hours
- ARTH 3020, Renaissance to Modern Art, 3 credit hours
- ARTH 3030, Contemporary Art and Design, 3 credit hours
- ARTH 4800, Design Practicum, 4 credit hours
- ARTS 2010, Introduction to Drawing, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 2011, Two-Dimensional Design, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 2060, Intermediate Drawing, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 2100, Intro to Graphic Design, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 3110, Visual Design Studio, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 3114, Human Experience in Design, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 3310, Typography Studio, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 3420, Design Through Print Production, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 4114, Design for Digital Media, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 4310, Design for Print Media, 3 credit hours
- ARTS 4420, Design Methodology, 2 credit hours
- ARTS 4930, Comprehensive Projects I Preparation, 2 credit hours
- ARTS 4950, Comprehensive Projects II, 4 credit hours

## Proposed course sequence:

Freshman:

ARTS 2010, 2011; ARTH 2100, ARTS 2100, 2060

Sophomore:

ARTH 3010 or 3020, ARTS 3110, 3114; ARTH 3030, ARTS 3310, 3420

Junior:

ARTH 3010 or 3020, ARTS 4114, 4310; ARTS 4420

Senior:

ARTH 4800, ARTS 4930; ARTS 4950

- ARTH 3030, Contemporary Art and Design, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- ARTS 4950, Comprehensive Projects, is a Speaking Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.

Students majoring in Visual Communication Design at Anderson University learn how to design for a variety of media. However, design is not just about what you make; it's also about how you think. From the first sketch to the final pixel, we encourage you to develop both skill and understanding by using critical, creative thinking to solve the challenges of visual communication.
