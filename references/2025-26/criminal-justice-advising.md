## Criminal Justice Major (34 credit hours)

## Major Requirements:

- CRIM 2510, The Nature of Crime and Social Deviance, 4 credit hours
- CRIM 2520, Introduction to Criminal Justice, 3 credit hours
- CRIM 3110, Criminal Law and Process, 4 credit hours
- CRIM 4810, Criminal Justice Internship I, 4 credit hours
- CRIM 4900, Criminal Justice Seminar, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2100, American National Government, 3 credit hours OR SOCI 2010, Introduction to Sociology, 3 credit hours
- SOCI 2020, Social Problems, 3 credit hours
- SOCI 3700, Introduction to Social Research, 4 credit hours
- 6 hours from additional CRIM courses

Students who wish to take additional coursework providing more breadth and depth in the field should consult with their advisor concerning the appropriate courses.

## Proposed course sequence:

Freshman:

CRIM 2520, SOCI 2020

Sophomore:

POSC 2100 or SOCI 2010; CRIM 2510

Junior:

CRIM 3110, SOCI 3700, CRIM Elective

Senior:

CRIM 4810, 4900, CRIM Elective

- CRIM 2510, The Nature of Crime and Social Deviance, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- CRIM 4900, Criminal Justice Seminar, is a Speaking Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- POSC 2100, American National Government, is a Civic Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts.
- SOCI 2020, Social Problems, is a Social &amp; Behavioral Ways of Knowing course in the Liberal Arts Program.
- SOCI 3700, Introduction to Social Research, is a Writing Intensive course in the Liberal Arts Program.

The criminal justice major at Anderson University prepares students to enter employment in fields related to law enforcement, courts and corrections, victim advocacy and assistance, and provides a foundation for graduate study. Coursework in the criminal justice program focuses on the social science of criminology and criminal justice.
