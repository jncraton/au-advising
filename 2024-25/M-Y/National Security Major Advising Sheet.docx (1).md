## National Security Major (44 credit hours)

The national security major is designed to develop graduates with the practical knowledge, technical skills, and professional ethics from a Christian-faith perspective, to serve on the front lines in defending the nation, our communities, and institutions from threats, foreign and domestic, in both the physical and cyber realms.

## Major Requirements:

Foundational Courses (16 credit hours):

- POSC 2020, Introduction to World Politics, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2100, American National Government, 3 credit hours (fulfills Civic Ways of Knowing)
- POSC 2200, Public Policy, 3 credit hours
- POSC 2400, Political Science Research Methods, 3 credit hours (fulfills Writing Intensive)
- MATH 2120, Introductory Statistics with Applications, 4 credit hours (fulfills Quantitative Reasoning)

## National Security Policy Courses (9 credit hours) from:

- POSC 3300, International Security, 3 credit hours
- POSC 3310, Political Violence and Terrorism, 3 credit hours (fulfills Writing Intensive)
- POSC/CRIM 3350, Homeland Security, 3 credit hours
- POSC 3370, Intelligence and Security Studies, 3 credit hours (fulfills Speaking Intensive)

## Ethics Course (3 credit hours):

- PHIL/RLGN 3250, Ethics and Morality for Professionals, 3 credit hours (fulfills Christian Ways of Knowing)
