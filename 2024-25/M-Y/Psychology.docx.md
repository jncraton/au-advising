## Psychology Major (30 credit hours)

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

Psychology is the study of behavior and mental processes. It crosses many disciplines including biology, sociology and religion. Students select to major or minor in psychology in order to prepare for working with people. By the time they graduate from the program, they will be prepared for graduate work in psychiatric social work, professional training in clinical or counseling psychology, working with families, personnel work in industry, pre-ministry, careers in the not-for-profit sector, working with handicapped individuals, or for careers in teaching and research.

## Major Requirements:

7 credit hours from:

- PSYC 2000, General Psychology, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 2010, Research Methods in Psychology, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 4900, Professional Issues for the Psychologist, 1 credit hour

15 credit hours from 3000 and 4000 level classes, at least 12 (of these 15 hours) must be from:

- PSYC 3010, Social Psychology, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 3030, Psychiatric Rehabilitation, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 3040, Group and Institutional Dynamics, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 3060, The Emotions, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 3100, Human Brain Dysfunction, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 3120, Abnormal Psychology, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 3200, Living the Christian Faith in a Post-Christian World, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 3210, Behavioral Neuroscience, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 3240, Experimental Design, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 3310, Psychological Tests, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 3330, Cognitive Psychology, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 3400, Memory, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 3450, Health Psychology, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 4030, Counseling and Psychotherapy, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 4100, Leadership and Training Skills, 2 credit hours
- PSYC 4510, Capstone Research I, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 4520, Capstone Research II, 2 credit hours
- PSYC 4650, Independent Study/Research Project, 1-4 credit hours (arranged)

The remaining 8 hours from any PSYC captioned course not previously taken, which may include those listed above and/or:

- PSYC 2100, Interpersonal Relationships, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 2110, Educational Psychology, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 2440, Applied Statistics and Introduction to Research, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 2510, Developmental Psychology, 4 credit hours
- PSYC 2850, Practicum in Leadership Skills, 2 credit hours
- PSYC 3220, Organizational Behavior and Theory, 3 credit hours
- PSYC 3500, Thought Life and Spiritual Growth, 2 credit hours

NOTE: All students must complete 120 total credit hours to graduate from Anderson University.
