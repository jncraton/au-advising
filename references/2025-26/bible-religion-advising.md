## Bible &amp; Religion Major (36 credit hours; Suggested Four Year Plan)* 2018-2019

## Major Requirements:

- BIBL 2000, Introduction to the Bible, 3 credit hours (offered each semester)
- BIBL 2050, Methods in Biblical Exegesis, 3 credit hours (offered semester two)**
- RLGN 2000, Introduction to Religion, 3 credit hours (offered semester one)
- RLGN 2060, History of Christianity, 3 credit hours (offered semester one)
- RLGN 2130, Introduction to Christian Ethics, 3 credit hours (offered semester one)**
- RLGN 2150, Hermeneutics: The Practice of Interpreting, 3 credit hours (offered semester one)**
- RLGN 3040, Introduction to Christian Theology, 3 credit hours (offered semester two)**
- RLGN 3320, A World of Religions, 3 credit hours (offered semester one [online]; offered semester two)***
- RLGN 3300, Beginnings of Christianity, 3 credit hours (offered semester one)**
- Upper Division Bible or Religion Course 3 credit hours
- Hebrew Bible Exegetical Course, 3 credit hours
- New Testament Exegetical Course, 3 credit hours
- **BIBL 2000 is a prerequisite for this course

***Upper division standing required

## Proposed course sequence:

Freshman:

BIBL 2000, RLGN 2000; BIBL 2050

Sophomore:  RLGN 2060, RLGN 2150; RLGN 3040

Junior:

RLGN 2130, RLGN 3000; DCM Exegetical Course; RLGN 3320, DCM Exegetical Course

Senior:

Upper Division Elective in either BIBL or RLGN

Courses in this major are drawn from the related areas of theology, Bible, and religion as students explore the biblical sources of Christian religion and its theology, practice, and relation to life and culture.  This major is especially valuable for pre-seminary students.
