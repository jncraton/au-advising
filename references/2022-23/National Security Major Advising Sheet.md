## National Security Major (44 credit hours)

The national security major prepares students for careers in public service and the lifelong learning necessary for success at the highest levels of government. The interdisciplinary curriculum is designed so graduates will demonstrate an understanding of the issues and forces driving global events and statecraft, develop competence in analytical research and communication skills, learn to identify policy problems, and articulate informed responses to challenges one might confront in public service. From a Christian faith perspective, students will express an understanding of and appreciation for the ethical constraints on state and individual behavior.
